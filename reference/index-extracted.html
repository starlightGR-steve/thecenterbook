Here's Why We Built This

She didn't open her center to fight with software.

Our first client runs a franchise tutoring center with over 300 students. She got into education for the kids, but found herself spending more time managing broken tools than working with families.

She wasn't on paper. She'd already invested in real platforms: all-in-one marketing tools, funnel builders, CRM systems that promised to handle everything. Thousands of dollars across multiple platforms.

But every platform had the same fundamental flaw: one-to-one data relationships. One parent email, one child. A mom with three kids needed three separate accounts. That's not how families work. No amount of custom fields could fix a structural data problem.

The marketing tools were great at selling online courses. The CRM systems were built for sales pipelines. None of them understood that a tutoring center has 300 students walking through a physical door. They were digital-first tools being forced into a physical-operations world.

So we built it differently. The Center Book starts with the database: real multi-to-multi relationships where one parent connects to multiple children, billing and communication contacts are separate, and every piece of the platform reads from the same source of truth.

The problems we kept seeing

Paper sign-in sheets and manual check-inNo instant access to medical alerts or session history
No real-time classroom visibilityNo way to see progress without walking the room
Pickup texts from personal phonesNo automation, no consent tracking, no records
Student data scattered across systemsSome in spreadsheets, some undocumented
Every system required one email per childFamilies forced into duplicate accounts
The common thread?
Great educators spending their energy on tools instead of students
